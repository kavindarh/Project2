REVOLUTION IN PROGRESS

Next Generation Sequencing has undoubtedly changed human lives reshaping diagnosis and treatment, enabling impressive scientific as well as novel achievements. With such cutting edge technology providing deeper insights into decoding mysteries of life, we have reached the era where International organizations are sequencing thousands of cancer genomes to find novel genetic changes, and individuals are paying for genome-wide association studies in hopes of preventing diseases to which they are predisposed.

Next Generation Sequencing has brought about a very rapid revolution in the scientific world and is a multidisciplinary, multi- institutional, international approach that is rapidly on its way to replacing the current molecular diagnostic gold standard, Sanger sequencing, a technology developed in the 1970s. [2] This technology has contributed to the transformation of genomic research and medicine, and is poised to have a similar impact in every field of biological research. With such innovative technology, healthcare providers around the world have now become able to perform rapid disease diagnosis while identifying the most efficacious drugs depending upon the genetic make-up of each patient. It also helps to identify genetic basis of all inherited diseases accurately discriminating between normal and faulty gene copies of humans. In addition, the ability to acquire affordable sequence data has enabled Next Generation Sequencing technology to expand beyond the confines of large research centers into many smaller laboratories worldwide. Having delivered on its promise of sequencing DNA at unprecedented speed, with unmatched accuracy and scalability Next Generation Sequencing has broaden the horizons of genomic research enabling researchers to decipher DNA sequences overcoming inherent limitations of traditional microbiological and diagnostic techniques. This ability has catalyzed a vast array of important biological breakthroughs advancing scientific fields from human disease research to agriculture and food processing. With Credence Genomics, Sri Lanka now welcomes Next Generation Sequencing for the first time in its history.Being the first and the only commercial Next Generation Sequencing provider, Credence Genomics is looking forward to leap beyond the boundaries, leveraging genomic research in the country. Based on a unique platform, the company offers high quality sequencing services in relation to both diagnostic and research areas to any individual at competitive value and pricing.With the efforts and essence of the right combination of technology and human resources put together, Credence Genomics is ready to deliver real-time genetic analysis services to their customers and invites all potential stakeholders to reap the benefits they offer.


FAR BEYOND THE NAKED EYE

Each human body plays host to a microbial population which is both numerically vast (at around 1014 microbial cells) and phenomenally diverse (over 1,000 species) [1] and play a crucial role in human health. Further to that microbes also play essential roles in many ecological niches at diverse stages, each being highly important for every form of life on earth. Different industries utilize the potential of these miniature forms of life as primary resources in order to enhance the end product quality and quantity. Study of microbial populations hence has come to limelight as many countries probe their diversity for more innovative and substantial achievements in scientific, medical and production environments.
