When considering accuracy, the conventional methods of detecting microbial species unavoidably claim a 20% rate of error in results. Since of late, DNA sequencing techniques have permitted to study whole populations of microorganisms in a given environment without the need to culture any of them in a petri dish.

Credence New Born Screening (CredneceNBS)

Screening children for inborn errors of metabolism using blood spots soon after birth (New Born Screening) has been in use for nearly 50 years. The biochemical tests that are done are time consuming and cumbersome. CredenceNBS, based on next generation sequencing, enables simultaneous testing of 28 genes responsible for 46 inborn errors of metabolism.

Sample to be tested: Blood

CredenceCarrier
